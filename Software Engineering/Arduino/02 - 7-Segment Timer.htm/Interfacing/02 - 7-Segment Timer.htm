7-Segment timer

Now that you know how to send a signal to a single LED, you also know how to send a signal to the pins on a 7-segment display.

Wiring

You will need an Arduino, a 7-segment display, two 220Ω resistors, jumper wires.

  1. Connect one jumper wire to the ground port on the Arduino and the other end of the wire to the ground terminal on the breadboard.
  2. Connect one jumper wire to the 5V port on the Arduino and the other end of the wire to the power terminal on the breadboard.
  3. Use resistors to connect the power terminal on the breadboard to pins 3 & 11 on the 7-segment display
  4. Connect pin 2 on the Arduino to pin 1 on the 7-segment display
  5. Connect pin 3 on the Arduino to pin 2 on the 7-segment display
  6. Connect pin 4 on the Arduino to pin 5 on the 7-segment display
  7. Connect pin 5 on the Arduino to pin 6 on the 7-segment display
  8. Connect pin 6 on the Arduino to pin 8 on the 7-segment display
  9. Connect pin 7 on the Arduino to pin 9 on the 7-segment display
  10. Connect pin 8 on the Arduino to pin 10 on the 7-segment display

Programming

  1. Open a new sketch
  2. In the Void Setup method set your pins (i.e. pinMode (2, OUTPUT);)
  3. In the Void Setup method set all of your pins output to high (i.e. digitalWrite (2, HIGH);)
  4. In the Void Loop method create digital writes for each number with a delay between each:
    • For the number 1:
      • digitalWrite (8, LOW);
      • digitalWrite (6, LOW);
      • delay (1000);
      • digitalWrite (8, HIGH);
      • digitalWrite (6, HIGH);
  5. Once you have finished setting all of the combinations in order to display numbers 0-9 verify your program
  6. Once your code is verified, Upload your program.

Advanced Timer

You can also use the Arduino to control a Dual 7-Segment display. It works the exact same way for programming, the wiring is just a little difference.

  1. Connect one jumper wire to the 5V port on the Arduino and the other end of the wire to the power terminal on the breadboard.
  2. Connect Pin 2 on the Arduino to Pin 1 on the Display.
  3. Connect Pin 3 on the Arduino to Pin 2 on the Display.
  4. Connect Pin 4 on the Arduino to Pin 3 on the Display.
  5. Connect Pin 5 on the Arduino to Pin 4 on the Display.
  6. Connect Pin 6 on the Arduino to Pin 5 on the Display.
  7. Connect Pin 7 on the Arduino to Pin 6 on the Display.
  8. Connect Pin 8 on the Arduino to Pin 7 on the Display.
  9. Connect Pin 9 on the Arduino to Pin 8 on the Display.
  10. Connect Pin 10 on the Arduino to Pin 9 on the Display.
  11. Connect Pin 11 on the Arduino to Pin 10 on the Display.
  12. Connect Pin 12 on the Arduino to Pin 11 on the Display.
  13. Connect a resistor to Pin 12 on the Display.
  14. Connect a resistor to Pin 13 on the Display.
  15. Connect Pin A1 on the Arduino to Pin 14 on the Display.
  16. Connect Pin A2 on the Arduino to Pin 15 on the Display.
  17. Connect Pin A3 on the Arduino to Pin 16 on the Display.
  18. Connect Pin A4 on the Arduino to Pin 17 on the Display.
  19. Connect Pin A5 on the Arduino to Pin 18 on the Display.
