the blink test

The first test for any Arduino programmer is the blink test. The blink test in Arduino is similar to the Hello World for a computer programmer; it allows you to learn about basic setup, wiring, programming, and output. The first step the wiring.

The Arduino Board

The Arduino is broken into two halves. On the left are the power controls for the circuit and the digital inputs. On the left side are the basic analog outputs.

Wiring Setup

  1. For the purposes of this example you will require an Arduino, a breadboard, a LED, a 220Ω resistor, and jumper wires.
  2. Connect one end of a jumper wire to the 5V terminal on the left side of the Arduino and the other end of the wire to the breadboard ground terminal.
  3. Connect one end of a jumper wire to the ground terminal on the left side of the Arduino and the other end of the wire to the breadboard power terminal.
  4. Connect one end of a jumper wire to Pin 13 on the right (Digital) side of the Arduino and the other end of the wire to the breadboard.
  5. Connect the positive wire of the LED to the same strip on the breadboard as the last jumper wire and the negative wire of the LED one row below.
  6. Connect a resistor from the negative end of the LED to the ground terminal on the breadboard.

  1. Change the amount of time the LED is off to 1 second. (Leaving the amount of time the LED is on at 1⁄2 second.)

  2. Change the pin to which the LED is connected from pin 13 to pin 2. (Note that both the circuit AND the program must be changed.)
