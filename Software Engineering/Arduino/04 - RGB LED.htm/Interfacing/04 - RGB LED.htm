rgb led

You know what’s even more fun than a blinking LED? Changing colors with one LED. RGB, or red-green-blue, LEDs have three different colour emitting diodes that can be combined to create all sorts of colors. In this circuit, you’ll learn how to use an RGB LED to create unique color combinations. Depending on how bright each diode is, nearly any color is possible! 

Wiring Setup

For the purposes of this example you will require an Arduino, a breadboard, a RGB LED, three 330Ω resistors, and jumper wires.

  1. An RGB LED is actually three LEDs (red, green, and blue) in one package. When you run them at different brightnesses, the red, green and blue mix to form new colours.
  2. Starting at the flattened edge of the flange on the LED, the pins are ordered RED, COMMON, GREEN, BLUE.
  3. Place the LED into the breadboard making sure that all four legs are separated.
  4. Connect RED to a 220 Ohm resistor. Connect the other end of the resistor to Arduino digital pin 9.
  5. Connect COMMON pin to GND.
  6. Connect GREEN to a 220 Ohm resistor. Connect the other end of the resistor to Arduino digital pin 10.
  7. Connect BLUE to a 220 Ohm resistor. Connect the other end of the resistor to Arduino digital pin 11.
