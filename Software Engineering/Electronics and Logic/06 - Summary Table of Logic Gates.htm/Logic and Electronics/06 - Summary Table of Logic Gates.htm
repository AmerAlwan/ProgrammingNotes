Converting Expressions

Converting boolean expressions to logic diagrams is the most challenging conversion because it requires a very good understanding of order of operation.  Below is the order of operations used in this conversion.

  1. BRACKETS
  2. NOT's
  3. NAND's
  4. NOR's
  5. AND's
  6. OR's

The “A,” “B,” and “C” input signals are assumed to be provided from switches, sensors, or perhaps other gate circuits. Where these signals originate is of no concern.

For example: Y = C (A + B)

STEP 1: identify the types of gates involved.

        C x () indicates an AND gate    

        A + B indicates an OR gate      

STEP 2: Work through the equation working from the top left down and to the right.

Converting Diagrams

To convert from a logic circuit diagram to a boolean expression we start by listing our inputs at the correct place and process the inputs through the gates, one gate at a time, writing the result at each gate’s output. When creating the equation we begin with "Y" and work our way back to the start of the circuit.
