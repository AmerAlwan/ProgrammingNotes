ohm's law and resistors

The following calculations are used:
  • Voltage = Current x Resistance
  • Resistance = Voltage ÷ Current
    • resistance is measure in ohms
    • Ω = R
  • Current = Voltage ÷ Resistance
    • current is measured in Amps
  • Power = Current x Voltage
    • power is measured in watts

Questions

1) A light bulb is operated by a 3v battery. When the switch is closed (the circuit is turned on) the bulb draws 0.075 Amps from the battery. What is the resistance of the bulb?
2) A toy car with an electric motor draws 0.25 Amps for the supply. It has an effective resistance of 60 Ohms. What is the voltage of the supply?
3) When starting a car, a current of 80 Amps is drawn from the 12v car battery. What is the voltage of the supply?
4) To defrost the rear windshield of a car, a heating element has an electrical current flowing through it. This is connected to the 12v battery. The power dissipated through the heating element is 36 watts.
a) What is the current flowing though the element?
b) What is the resistance of the heating element?
5) A solenoid for a pinball table has a rating of 45 watts and a resistance of 5 ohms.
a) What is the current flowing though the solenoid?
b) What is the correct voltage to supply the solenoid?

What are resistors?

Resistors are small metal parts that limit the amount of electricity that can flow through a circuit.
